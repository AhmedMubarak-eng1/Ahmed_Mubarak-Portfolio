Steel Manufacturing / Water Treatment

US Steel HDGL – Wastewater Treatment Control System

JNE Group (Client: US Steel) May – Sept 2025

Overview

Engineered the complete control system design for US Steel's Hot-Dip Galvanizing Line (HDGL) wastewater treatment project — one of the most technically demanding deliverables during my co-op at JNE Group. The project required translating a complex industrial P&ID into a complete, fabrication-ready control system package for a major North American steel producer.

What Was Built

  • Performed detailed P&ID analysis to understand the full process flow and define all instrumentation, control points, and interlocks
  • Produced a comprehensive 368+ point I/O list covering all sensors, actuators, signals, safety interlocks, and control loops
  • Designed complete AutoCAD Electrical schematics for the control system including power distribution, control circuits, and motor control
  • Developed detailed panel layouts for fabrication — ensuring accurate component placement, wiring routes, and cable management
  • Supported Factory Acceptance Testing (FAT) to verify panel functionality and I/O integrity prior to site deployment
  • Assisted with on-site commissioning at the US Steel facility, verifying field wiring and system startup
  • Ensured full compliance with CSA and ISA standards throughout the design and documentation process

Results & Impact

Delivered a complete, fabrication-ready control system package that enabled accurate panel manufacturing and a seamless commissioning process. The 368+ point I/O list and schematic package were delivered with zero rework required during FAT — demonstrating the accuracy and quality of the design work.
