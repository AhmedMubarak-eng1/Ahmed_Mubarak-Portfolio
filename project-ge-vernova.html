GE Vernova – 2024 Spring Outage Season (5 Power Stations)

GE Vernova (General Electric) Jan – Sept 2024

Overview

Deployed as a Field Engineer during Ontario's 2024 Spring Outage Season, supporting the repair, upgrade, and maintenance of gas turbines, steam turbines, and generator systems across five power stations. Operating in high-pressure, ~90% travel environments, I served as an active member of the execution team alongside senior GE engineers, TFAs, millwright crews, and generator specialists.

What Was Built

  • Led crews during turbine disassembly — provided technical procedures and direction to millwright and mechanical crews
  • Supervised clearance measurements during turbine reassembly to ensure compliance with OEM tolerance specifications
  • Collaborated with TFAs, generator specialists on diagnostic testing, generator rewinds, and heavy lifting operations
  • Supported Controls TFAs with HMI upgrades using GE Cimplicity — including testing of thermocouples, control valves, and alarm systems
  • Prepared field measurements, machining drawings, and inspection reports verifying OEM standards across five sites
  • Actively participated in internal and external planning meetings — helped build outage project plans, coordinated manpower, and ensured tools and parts were delivered on time
  • Maintained rigorous safety leadership — enforced LOTO procedures, confined space entry protocols, PPE compliance, and daily safety briefings
  • Contributed to multi-million-dollar project scheduling and vendor coordination to keep outages on track

Results & Impact

Contributed to the successful and on-schedule completion of multi-million-dollar outage projects across five Ontario power stations. System upgrades including HMI and DCS improvements enhanced monitoring, reliability, and safety across all facilities. Gained hands-on experience in one of Canada's most demanding field engineering environments.
