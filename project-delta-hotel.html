Delta (Marriott) Ottawa Hotel – Dual Air Compressor BAS

Midwest Services Canada Jan – March 2025 Ottawa, Ontario

Overview

This project represents one of the most complete engineering deliverables of my career to date — a full end-to-end Building Automation System (BAS) for the dual air compressor infrastructure at the Delta Ottawa Hotel, a Marriott-brand property in the heart of Canada's capital. From initial requirements gathering and electrical design through hardware procurement, panel fabrication, on-site commissioning, and final ESA/ESAFE certification, I owned every phase of this project while working part-time at Midwest Services Canada.

The hotel's compressed air system is a critical utility — powering pneumatic door actuators, HVAC dampers, and maintenance tooling across multiple floors. The legacy system lacked any intelligent control, leaving maintenance staff with no visibility into compressor health, runtime hours, or pressure faults. This project replaced that with a modern, fully automated, and remotely monitored solution built around a Siemens LOGO! PLC and a custom CSA-compliant control panel.

Runtime Tracking

Each compressor has an independent hour meter tracked by the LOGO! PLC. When either unit accumulates enough runtime to trigger a maintenance milestone, the system automatically flags the event and notifies engineering staff — eliminating calendar-based guesswork and extending service intervals to actual need.

What Was Built

  • Designed the full system architecture — power distribution, control circuits, motor protection schemes, and protection coordination — documented in AutoCAD Electrical to fabrication-ready standards
  • Programmed a Siemens LOGO! PLC (6ED1052-1MD08-0BA2) for automatic lead/lag compressor sequencing, manual override modes, runtime hour metering, and preventive maintenance scheduling tied to real operating hours rather than fixed calendar intervals
  • Integrated advanced alarm handling — pressure drop detection, low pressure warnings, abnormal runtime flags, and compressor fault conditions — with automated alerts delivered directly to hotel engineering staff
  • Automated daily engineer reports providing real-time visibility of system health, current pressure status, individual runtime hours, and upcoming maintenance milestones — replacing manual log sheets entirely
  • Designed and built a CSA-compliant custom control panel including 600V power distribution, Weidmüller 24VDC SMPS, Siemens Sirius motor starters, terminal block arrays, and all I/O field wiring — assembled to ESA wiring standards
  • Built the operator interface on the panel door: illuminated pushbuttons (ON/WARNING/FAULT) for each compressor, MAN/OFF/AUTO selector switches, dual E-STOP mushroom heads, and a global POWER ON indicator — providing clear at-a-glance system status
  • Managed full procurement and supplier coordination — sourced all panel hardware, instruments, and electrical components to spec within project budget and schedule
  • Commissioned the system on-site at the Delta Ottawa Hotel mechanical room, verified field wiring continuity, tested all control sequences under live conditions, and delivered hands-on training to hotel engineering staff on operation, fault response, and maintenance procedures
  • Achieved ESA/ESAFE certification — the complete installation passed Ontario electrical safety inspection, meeting all applicable codes for commercial building electrical systems

Results & Impact

The Delta Ottawa Hotel received a fully certified, production-grade automation system that transformed a completely manual compressor room into an intelligent, self-monitoring utility. Unplanned downtime was significantly reduced through proactive pressure monitoring and fault alerting. Compressor service intervals were extended by shifting from calendar-based maintenance to actual runtime-hour tracking, saving the hotel unnecessary service costs. Engineering staff gained complete real-time visibility via automated daily health reports — eliminating manual checks and paper logs.

The client received a complete documentation handover package — including AutoCAD electrical drawings, PLC logic descriptions, I/O lists, and a maintenance procedures manual — ensuring the system can be confidently operated and supported for years without reliance on the original engineer.
