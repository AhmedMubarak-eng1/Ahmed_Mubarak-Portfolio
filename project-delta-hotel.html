Delta (Marriott) Ottawa Hotel – Dual Air Compressor BAS

Midwest Services Canada Jan – March 2025 Ottawa, Ontario

Overview

This project represents one of the most complete engineering deliverables of my career to date — a full end-to-end Building Automation System (BAS) for the dual air compressor infrastructure at the Delta Ottawa Hotel, a Marriott-brand property in the heart of Canada's capital. From initial requirements gathering and electrical design through hardware procurement, panel fabrication, on-site commissioning, and final ESA/ESAFE certification, I owned every phase of this project while working part-time at Midwest Services Canada.

The hotel's compressed air system is a critical utility — powering pneumatic door actuators, HVAC dampers, and maintenance tooling across multiple floors. The legacy system lacked any intelligent control, leaving maintenance staff with no visibility into compressor health, runtime hours, or pressure faults. This project replaced that with a modern, fully automated solution built around a Siemens LOGO! PLC and a custom CSA-compliant panel.

System Design & Panel Build

The project began with a complete electrical design package — power distribution, motor protection, control circuits, and protection coordination schematics — all produced in AutoCAD Electrical to fabrication-ready standards. Every component was specified, procured, and assembled into a CSA-compliant custom control panel built to Ontario Electrical Code requirements.

The panel houses a Weidmüller PROeco 24VDC SMPS, Siemens LOGO! PLC, four Siemens Sirius motor starters, a MCB for incoming protection, and a full DIN-rail terminal block array for clean field wiring terminations.

Panel interior — Weidmüller SMPS, Siemens LOGO! PLC, Sirius motor starters, and terminal block arrays

PLC Logic & Automation

The Siemens LOGO! PLC was programmed for automatic lead/lag compressor sequencing — rotating the lead unit to balance wear between both machines. Manual override allows hotel staff to force either compressor on or off for maintenance without disrupting the other. Independent runtime hour counters track actual operating time per unit, triggering preventive maintenance alerts at configurable hour thresholds.

Alarm logic monitors system pressure continuously — flagging low pressure conditions, abnormal runtime patterns, and compressor faults, with automated notifications delivered directly to hotel engineering staff.

Operator Interface

The panel door was designed as a clean, intuitive operator interface. Three-colour indicator lights (ON / WARNING / FAULT) give instant status for each compressor. MAN / OFF / AUTO selector switches allow mode changes without opening the panel. Dual emergency-stop mushroom heads provide independent safe-stop capability for each machine. All devices are clearly labelled and wired to numbered terminal blocks inside.

Finished operator panel — fully installed and live at the Delta Ottawa Hotel mechanical room

On-Site Commissioning

After panel fabrication was complete, I travelled to the Delta Ottawa Hotel to commission the system on-site. All field wiring was verified for continuity and correct termination, every control sequence was tested under live conditions, and both compressors were run through automatic and manual modes to confirm correct operation.

Commissioning included pressure sensor calibration, alarm threshold verification, and a full fault simulation to confirm every warning and fault condition triggered correctly. Following commissioning, I delivered hands-on training to hotel engineering staff — covering daily operation, fault response procedures, and how to read the LOGO! display for runtime status and system health.

On-site commissioning — laptop connected to the LOGO! PLC via Ethernet for live program monitoring

Runtime Hour Tracking

One of the most valuable features of the system is the independent runtime hour counter for each compressor. Rather than calendar-based service intervals, the LOGO! PLC tracks actual operating hours — alerting engineering staff when either unit approaches its next maintenance milestone. This prevents both under-servicing (which causes failures) and over-servicing (which wastes budget). The images below show both counters live shortly after initial commissioning.

Results & Impact

The Delta Ottawa Hotel received a fully certified, production-grade automation system that transformed a completely manual compressor room into an intelligent, self-monitoring utility. Unplanned downtime was significantly reduced through proactive pressure monitoring and fault alerting. Compressor service intervals were extended by shifting from calendar-based maintenance to actual runtime-hour tracking — saving unnecessary service costs while ensuring no unit is ever run past its due date.

The client received a complete documentation handover package — AutoCAD electrical drawings, PLC logic descriptions, I/O lists, and a maintenance procedures manual — ensuring the system can be confidently operated and supported for years without reliance on the original engineer.
