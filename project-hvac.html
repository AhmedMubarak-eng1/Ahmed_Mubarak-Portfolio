Framing note

This page is intentionally cleaner and more mature in tone. The goal is to help the project feel relevant to employers instead of sounding like a casual side experiment.

What was delivered

  • Designed a small-scale HVAC automation concept around the Schneider M172 platform, with attention to panel layout, I/O structure, and sequence behaviour.
  • Worked through heating and cooling logic, operational monitoring ideas, and BAS-friendly system organization.
  • Used the project to deepen practical understanding of controller setup, equipment interfacing, and clean technical documentation habits.

Why this project matters

  • Even as a learning project, it helps position you for building automation roles because it shows initiative and targeted skill development.
  • It broadens your website beyond one industry and shows you can adapt to different control environments and controller ecosystems.
  • It is especially useful when presented as a disciplined engineering exercise instead of just a hobby build.

How I would position this work professionally

This page is written to sound more like a real engineering case study and less like a school assignment. That matters because the goal is not only to show what was built, but also to show how you think, how you execute, and how you create confidence.

Best signal: This project strengthens your brand as someone who can own technical scope, communicate clearly, and move work toward an operational result.
