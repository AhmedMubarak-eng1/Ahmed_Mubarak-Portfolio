About Me

Built for the field.
Driven by purpose.

I'm Ahmed Mubarak — an I&C Automation and Control Systems Engineer (EIT) currently at AECOM, one of the world's leading engineering firms, where I contribute to the design and delivery of electrical and automation systems for large-scale projects across energy, power generation, nuclear, defense, water/wastewater, and critical infrastructure.

My experience spans some of Canada's most demanding engineering environments — critical outage operations across five Ontario power stations with GE Vernova, including gas & steam turbine maintenance, generator overhauls, and HMI/SCADA upgrades. At JNE Group, I programmed PLC systems and designed control panels and electrical schematics for major steel producers including US Steel, Dofasco, STELCO, and Algoma. At Midwest Services Canada, I engineered a full building automation system for a Marriott hotel from design through commissioning. At Salford Group (Linamar), I designed control harnesses and vehicle control systems for agricultural machinery. Beyond industry, I served as an Automation Lab Research Assistant and Teaching Assistant at McMaster University for Advanced Allen-Bradley PLCs, control systems, and core engineering fundamentals.

I hold an active Bruce Power Security Clearance and Government of Canada Reliability & Nuclear Clearance — trusted to work on Canada's most sensitive nuclear and federal infrastructure. My work is driven by precision, accountability, and the ability to deliver under pressure — bringing a high level of reliability and performance to every system I design and deploy.
